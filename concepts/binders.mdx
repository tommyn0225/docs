A binder is your dedicated space for tracking a card collection. Think of it like a physical binder on your shelf — it holds a set of cards, has a name you choose, and belongs to one specific trading card game. Everything in Binder is organized around binders: you add cards to them, view your collection's value through them, and share them with others.

## Binder Limits

Each account supports **up to 3 binders**. This limit applies across all games combined, so plan accordingly before creating new binders.

<Tip>
  Since each binder is locked to one game, the natural approach is one binder per game — one for Magic: The Gathering, one for Pokémon, and one for One Piece. That fills your three slots and keeps each collection neatly separated.
</Tip>

## Games

When you create a binder, you choose which game it belongs to:

- **Magic: The Gathering**
- **Pokémon**
- **One Piece TCG**

This game selection is permanent for that binder — you cannot mix cards from different games in a single binder, and you cannot change the game after creation. If you want to track cards from a different game, create a new binder for it.

## Public vs. Private

All binders start as **private**, meaning only you can see them when logged in.

Toggling a binder to **public** generates a shareable link in the format:

```
/b/{binder-id}
```

Anyone with that link can view the binder and its contents — no account or login required. Public binders are read-only for visitors; they can browse your cards but cannot make any changes.

Switch a binder back to private at any time to immediately revoke public access.

## Binder Actions

From the binder settings, you can:

- **Rename** — Update the binder's display name whenever you like.
- **Toggle visibility** — Flip the binder between public and private.
- **Delete** — Permanently remove the binder from your account.

<Warning>
  Deleting a binder is irreversible. All holdings inside the binder — every card entry, quantity, and trade flag — are permanently deleted along with it. Export or note anything you want to keep before deleting.
</Warning>
